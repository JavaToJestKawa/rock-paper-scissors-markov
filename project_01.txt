Assignment (14 points): Rock–Paper–Scissors with Bayesian Learning and Hidden Markov Model
Implement a simulation of the Rock–Paper–Scissors game in Python in which one player follows a probabilistic strategy and the second player learns and predicts this strategy over time.

(10 points) Code should include:
One player whose strategy is defined by a transition matrix describing the probability of choosing the next move depending on the previous move.
A second player that predicts the opponent’s next move using observed transitions and updates the probabilities using a Bayesian update. The player should use a counter-move strategy that tries to beat the predicted move.
Simulation of at least 1000 rounds of the game.
Tracking and plotting the accumulated score over rounds.
Printing the learned transition matrix after the simulation.

Note: You should be prepared to answer questions about your code, including both the theoretical background and the programming aspects. The questions will refer directly to the code, so keep your implementation simple and clear.
